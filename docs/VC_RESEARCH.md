## Y Combinator - Requests for Startups (Spring 2026)

Source: https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs

### 1. Cursor for Product Managers
- Problem: Coding tools like Cursor help BUILD, but not decide WHAT to build
- Opportunity: AI tool for product discovery - upload customer interviews, usage data, ask "what should we build next?"
- Output: Feature outlines, UI changes, task breakdowns for coding agents
- **My fit:** HIGH - I could use this, and could build this

### 2. AI-Native Hedge Funds
- Problem: Big funds slow to adapt to AI
- Opportunity: Swarms of AI agents analyzing 10-Ks, earnings calls, SEC filings, making trades
- Not just bolting AI onto existing strategies - entirely new approaches
- **My fit:** MEDIUM - need domain expertise, regulatory complexity

### 3. AI-Native Agencies
- Problem: Agencies have low margins, hard to scale
- Opportunity: AI agencies with software margins - design, ads, legal
- Sell the finished product, not the software
- **My fit:** HIGH - EkoInk is kind of this model (AI + handwritten notes as a service)

### 4. Stablecoin Financial Services
- Problem: Gap between regulated finance and crypto
- Opportunity: DeFi benefits (yield, tokenized assets) with TradFi compliance
- Regulatory window opening (GENIUS Act, CLARITY Act)
- **My fit:** LOW - crypto complexity, regulatory risk

### 5. AI for Government
- Problem: Govt still prints and processes forms by hand
- Opportunity: AI to handle incoming applications, make govt more efficient
- Hard to sell to, but sticky once in
- **My fit:** MEDIUM - long sales cycles, need connections

### 6. AI Guidance for Physical Work
- Problem: Physical jobs can't be replaced by AI (yet)
- Opportunity: Real-time AI coaching via camera/glasses for field service, healthcare, manufacturing
- "I know Kung Fu" Matrix moment - instant skill acquisition
- **My fit:** LOW - requires hardware, physical integration

### 7. Large Spatial Models
- Problem: LLMs don't reason spatially
- Opportunity: Foundation models for 3D/physical world reasoning
- Could be next OpenAI-scale company
- **My fit:** LOW - massive R&D, not my strength

### 8. Infra for Government Fraud Hunters
- Problem: Fraud investigation is slow and manual
- Opportunity: AI tools for qui tam lawsuits, False Claims Act cases
- Accelerate whistleblower law firms, state AGs
- **My fit:** MEDIUM - interesting but need domain expertise

### 9. Make LLMs Easy to Train
- Problem: Training LLMs is painful - broken SDKs, GPU issues, data management
- Opportunity: APIs that abstract training, better dev environments for ML research
- **My fit:** MEDIUM - could build this, but crowded space

## Key Insights

1. **AI-native > AI-enhanced**: VCs want companies built from scratch on AI, not legacy + AI bolt-on

2. **Services with software margins**: The "AI agency" model is hot - do the work with AI, charge service prices

3. **Vertical AI wins**: Generic AI tools are saturated, vertical-specific (healthcare, legal, govt) still has room

4. **The "what to build" problem**: Coding is getting solved, product discovery is the next bottleneck

5. **AI workforce coordination**: Multiple agents working together is emerging theme (connects to Hive)
